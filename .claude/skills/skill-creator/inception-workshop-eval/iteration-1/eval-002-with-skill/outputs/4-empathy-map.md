# Step 4: Empathy Map

## Primary Persona: Sarah Chen (Conference Organizer)

### SAYS
- "I need to get this done quickly - the deadline is approaching!"
- "How many submissions do I have so far?"
- "Can I see which reviewers haven't completed their reviews?"
- "I need to assign reviewers without conflicts"
- "The schedule needs to be conflict-free"

### THINKS
- "This is taking way too long"
- "What if I miss something important?"
- "I wish there was a better way to manage this"
- "Will the reviewers be happy with their assignments?"
- "I need to look professional to my stakeholders"

### DOES
- Manages multiple spreadsheets simultaneously
- Sends dozens of reminder emails
- Manually checks for reviewer conflicts
- Creates schedules in Google Calendar or similar tools
- Coordinates with co-organizers via email

### FEELS
- **Overwhelmed** by the volume of submissions
- **Anxious** about making mistakes in assignments
- **Frustrated** with manual processes
- **Relieved** when things go smoothly
- **Proud** when the event runs well

---

## Empathy Map Insights

### Key Pain Points
1. **Time Pressure**: Manual processes consume days of work
2. **Error Risk**: Manual conflict checking is prone to mistakes
3. **Visibility**: Hard to track progress across multiple dimensions
4. **Communication**: Coordinating with reviewers is tedious

### Opportunity Areas
1. **Automation**: Auto-assign reviewers with conflict detection
2. **Dashboard**: Single view of all submissions and review status
3. **Notifications**: Automated reminders and status updates
4. **Schedule Generation**: One-click conflict-free schedule creation

### User Needs (Prioritized)
1. **Must Have**: Clear submission management and review tracking
2. **Should Have**: Automated reviewer assignment
3. **Could Have**: Advanced analytics and reporting
4. **Won't Have**: Full event management (registration, etc.)
